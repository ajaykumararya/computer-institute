Organ donation and Transplantation

  • 09 Aug 2019

 

Organ donation and Transplantation is one of the most remarkable medical success stories of this century.   Adesh Institute of Medical Sciences and Research, Bathinda has got the approval from Govt of Punjab to start Liver Transplantation.

Today i.e 09.08.2019 the institution organized a Lecture in collaboration with MOHAN Foundation Chennai for awareness of organ donation amongst   the faculty staff and students. A team of Mohan Foundation (Northern India) chapter visited Adesh University. The team made a presentation highlighting the importance and procedure of organ donation and give the awareness.  They told that Vital organs like heart, liver, kidneys, intestines, lungs and pancreas can be donated in case of brain death. However, other tissues like corneas, heart valves, skin bones etc can be donated in natural death. Organ like Kidney, Liver can also be donated by living donor. One can be an organ donor by singing a donor card from any hospital and NGO. If a person has a donor card, it means that he/she is willing  to donate organs upon death. We should remember that in our country even if one has signed the donor card, it is important to have the consensus of the family while signing the donor card. The donor card should be kept by everyone in his wallet as a proof of his/her wish to donate organs upon death. Dr

GPI Singh, Vice Chancellor, Dr Gurpreet Singh Gill, Medical Supdt (Admn),  Col. Jagdev Singh (Retd.), Registrar, Dr G L Sharma, Principal, AIMSR, Dr Avtar Singh Bansal, Medical Supdt and Mr Kulwant Singh, Deputy Registrar were present

ARRHYTHMIA 2019 (LITERARY CLUB)-Medical quiz (MEDIQ)

  • 09 Mar 2019
A quiz was organized in the Auditorium on 8th March 2019 by the Literary Committee for the students of AIMSR as a part of the ongoing Arrhythmia 2019. All the batches 2015, 2016, 2017 and 2018 had three representatives from each. The topic of the quiz was History of Medicine & Recent Advances. There were four rounds i.e. Guess Me round, Visual clue round, Unscramble round and Rapid fire round. All the students participated with full vigour and enthusiasm. The first prize was bagged by Batch 2016 while batch 2017 was the first runner up. The certificates and medals were awarded to the students by honble Vice Chancellor Dr GPI Singh, Dr Harkiran Kaur, Dean Faculty of Medical Sciences and Dr GL Sharma, Principal, AIMSR.

SAKSHAM - A step towards Gender Sensitization

  • 16 Nov 2015
Under the aegis of the Gender Sensitization Committee, AIMSR, an activity was organized on 27th September 2014. Students and Faculty members of AIMSR participated in the program. The highlights of the event were *Students and faculty members were appraised by Dr Jaswinder Kaur, Prof & Head Anatomy regarding the existence of gender bias across the society. She discussed the Articles of Constitution of India related to gender sensitization and sexual harassment. Male gender should be sensitized to respect the opposite gender. *A choreography Astitva was enacted by students of MBBS batches 2013 and 2014. They very candidly outlined the gender bias in acceptance and upbringing of female child since her birth. They however brought a positive note to the presentation by showing women empowerment. *Dr Annu Maheshwari Assoc Prof Pharmacology then spoke on Self defense for girls: Measure for safety.
